Pilot Project for Jamaica


Our pilot project will launch in the fall of 2015 with a two day conference to be held in Kingston, Jamaica, where we will announce the ShippingHub National Logistics Strategy (SNLS). In the days that follow, and with the assistance of our local partners and supporters, we will begin the implementation of the strategy, paving the way for Jamaica to transform into a logistics hub.

The Pilot Project for Jamaica will launch the first two elements of the SNLS: Automated Shipping Solutions with full track and trace capabilities; and ShippingHub BIG DATA with NAC and Visualization Tool. We have partnered with the School of Computing and Information Technology at the University of Technology, Jamaica (UTech SCIT) to measure the impact of our pilot project.

We are pleased that, in addition to our partners at UTech SCIT, we enjoy the support of the Jamaica Chamber of Commerce (JCC) and the Jamaica Business Development Corporation (JBDC).

Why Jamaica?

Ranked 70th on the World Bank’s Logistic Performance Index for 2014, Jamaica has significant barriers to economic growth. As with many smaller island nations, the costs to export and import goods are high, acting as barriers to entering new markets and driving up the costs of imports that may be necessary to improving and sustaining development efforts. However, the Jamaican government is dedicated to implementing the Jamaica Logistics Hub (JLH) initiative, and has secured a $50 million loan from the World Bank to pursue development goals. Because of these factors, our initiative is highly compatible with these efforts, and we have found friendly local partners to help deliver our project.

What will the ShippingHub National Logistics Strategy do for Jamaica in 12 months?


  • Empower local logistics service providers (LSP) with the technology to deliver automation and full Track and Trace capabilities.

  • Effectively train a work force on logistics automation and allow software engineers to develop unique applications to further improve on productivity.

  • Implement a central information infrastructure to securely store and share shipment data in order to permit consolidations and reduce transportation costs.

  • Reduce and control transportation costs for inbound (imports of raw materials, parts, industrial machinery, etc) and outbound (exports) shipments.

  • Promote and market local goods and food products for international markets.

  • Promote Trans-shipment Centres or Super DCs operated and managed by members of the local populace to allow inbound and outbound consolidations of goods and export crops.

  • Promote and facilitate farming cooperatives and gather production data from farmers to maximize production, market reach, and profit while minimizing spoilage.

SNLS is both scalable and duplicable. It will complement Jamaica’s Logistics Hub (JLH) initiative and will propel Jamaica into the role of regional logistics leader. With SNLS, we believe Jamaica can follow Singapore’s example and become the fourth global logistics node in one generation.
