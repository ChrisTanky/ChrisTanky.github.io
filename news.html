Trip Report - Kingston, Jamaica - July, 2014

Posted July 21, 2014

We had a very successful business trip to Kingston, Jamaica (July 9 - July 18).

On July 9th, 2014, we arrived in Kingston, Jamaica, to begin our string of meetings with ministries, private sector organizations, and higher learning institutions. Our primary objective was to receive feedback on the ShippingHub National Logistic Strategy (SNLS) and to receive their official support via written endorsements. We are delighted to report that our trip was an astounding success, going beyond our objectives.

Both the ministries and private sector organizations agreed to officially support us with written endorsements. They believe the National Logistic Strategy will address many of the challenges that plague Jamaicaâ€™s economy. These difficulties include lack of domestic imports, lack of exports, perishable crops, and inefficient infrastructure. They recognized that our innovative technology would increase local imports by transforming high-value perishables into export crops that will be competitive in both domestic and external markets. Furthermore, they acknowledged that we would stimulate success for small and medium size enterprises by providing the information infrastructure and shipping solutions to enable their businesses to flourish. Each organization is committed to our cause and will continually support us by offering their expertise when needed.

Prior to our trip to Jamaica, we had a meeting with representatives of the Centre for Global Engineering (CGEN of University of Toronto) who expressed interest in leading the research and design of the ShippingHub Pods and Trailers, and the Big Data technology. While we were in Jamaica, we met with representatives of two higher learning institutions, Mona Geoinformatics Institute of the University of West Indies and the University of Technology Jamaica, to possibly collaborate with CGEN. The meetings proved fruitful, as both institutions agreed to be involved. Furthermore, Executive James Moss-Solomon of the Mona School of Business and Management wants to send his students for a week-long internship to Canada to further understand the Big Data technology.
