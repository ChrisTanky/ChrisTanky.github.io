Direct-Trade-Route


The high cost of transportation for both inbound and outbound shipments continues to hinder economic development and trade for low and middle income countries. These countries must import most of their raw materials, parts and industrial products, with some of these products costing two to three times what North American consumers and businesses are accustomed to paying. This represents a major barrier to trade and development.

ShippingHub’s Direct-Trade-Route is a core component of our effort to lower this barrier.

By moving consolidated shipments of both imports and exports along dedicated trade routes, ShippingHub’s Direct Trade Route system will significantly lower both import and export transport costs. As costs are lowered, trade will increase, ultimately accelerating development.

ShippingHub will establish a web portal through which it will promote the goods exported by the developing country while also offering for sale to users of the system the desired industrial and lifestyle products identified by that country’s private sector. The users of the web portal may organize shipments for export or purchase goods for import from ShippingHub’s participating suppliers. The tool will allow users to see both the current shipping cost along with a predicted lower cost as items are consolidated into larger shipments for movement at both ends of the route.

Direct-Trade-Route is both scalable and duplicable, is a key part of our effort to ‘democratize’ global trade.
