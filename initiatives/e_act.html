e-ACT – Employment Agencies Collaboration and Training


ShippingHub Global understands that full implementation of the ShippingHub National Logistics Strategy requires people trained to use the technology and to understand their roles in the supply chain. The Employment Agencies Collaboration and Training initiative, or e-ACT, will provide the training and employment preparation required to operate in an automated logistics environment. In order to develop and deliver the highest quality training programs available, e-ACT aims to foster collaboration between publicly and privately funded employment agencies.

Initially, e-ACT will offer logistics-focused seminars to highlight the importance of efficient supply chain execution, as well as more detailed role-specific courses for individuals interested in seeking a long-term job in the distribution centre environment. These employment opportunities often include operating machines, equipment, and computer programs.

e-ACT’s ultimate goal is to make skill and employment training more accessible. We’re designing our seminars and courses so that individuals with mental and physical disabilities will also benefit from our training program and can be offered the same employment opportunities.

e-ACT has earned support from both private and public sectors organizations in Jamaica. The Jamaica Chamber of Commerce, Jamaica Business Development Corporation, Mona School of Business Management (UWI), University of Technology of Jamaica, Rural Agriculture Development Agency, Jamaica Exporter Association, and Mona GIS have unanimously identified e-ACT as the most impactful initiative of the ShippingHub National Logistics Strategy.

e-Act Services and Deliverables

  • A 2-hour seminar to promote effective Supply Chain Execution and its impact

  • Multiple detailed ½ or full-day training sessions to focus on various phases of Supply Chain Execution: Planning, Receiving, Order picking, packing and shipping, and more

  • A simulated distribution centre with software and hardware

  • Site-visits (where available) with classroom and on-site on-the-job training

  • E-Act web site through which registered users may obtain information on the latest industrial trends and access to constantly updated learning materials
