Pilot Project for Jamaica

Democratize Global Trade

High transportation costs to import and export raw materials, parts, goods and food crops remain the major impediment for middle and low-income countries to compete globally.

Low-income countries lacks commercial demand, competitive pressure, industry knowledge and more critically, affordability to automate to increase productivity, save costs and fuel economic growth.

"Internal logistics can and will help feed millions"

We believe improved internal logistics can and will help feed millions by improving the shelve life of perishables and to provide the opportunity for farmers to export such fresh produces directly to restaurants and grocery stores in the very near future.

The Statistics

There is no global food shortage. In 2010, the world produced 2.2 billion tons of cereals, more than 2.5 times its production five decades earlier. Food production outpaced population growth of 4 billion people, yet more than a billion people remain hungry or vulnerable to sharp changes in food prices. Why?

Currently, billions do not have an address* to receive mail, delivery and emergency services. Without addresses, trade is restricted to a local level; walking distance, Sunday markets!

Join our amazing journey to democratize and expand global trade!
